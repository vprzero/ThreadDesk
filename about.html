Our story

Built in Charleston,
for Charleston shops.

ThreadDesk started with one observation: the most talented seamstresses in the city were getting one-star reviews for problems that had nothing to do with their sewing.

The origin

We saw the problem firsthand.

Walking into alteration shops across Charleston, the pattern was the same everywhere. Phones ringing constantly. Paper tickets stacked on the counter. Customers waiting while the seamstress stopped mid-stitch to look something up.

And the reviews? Two and a half, three stars. "Hard to reach." "My order wasn't ready." "Didn't feel welcome." Meanwhile, the actual alterations? Flawless. The craft was never the problem.

The problem was that these women were being asked to run a modern customer-service operation with no tools at all.

The solution

Software that disappears into the background.

We didn't want to build another complicated dashboard that required a training course. We wanted to build something a 58-year-old woman running a shop alone could set up in five minutes and never think about again.

So that's what ThreadDesk is. You set your prices once. You tap one button when a customer walks in. Everything else — intake, payments, follow-ups, reviews — runs automatically.

It's not software you learn. It's software that learns your shop and runs it for you.
